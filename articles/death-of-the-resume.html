The Death of the Resume

By Timothy S. Krause

Your Domain Is Your Career

Traditional resumes are dying. In 2026, professionals increasingly use personal domain portfolios to showcase work, establish credibility, and control their professional narrative. This shift creates unprecedented opportunities for investors who understand career-focused domain value. At DomanID, we've tracked the rise of personal professional domains and helped investors build portfolios serving this growing market. This guide reveals why domains are replacing resumes and how to profit from this transformation.

Understanding the Resume Revolution

Multiple forces are killing traditional resumes:

Digital Portfolio Preference

  • Employers prefer seeing actual work over resume claims
  • Personal domains showcase projects, case studies, testimonials
  • Dynamic content updates versus static resume documents
  • Rich media (video, interactive demos) impossible on resumes

Search and Discovery

  • Recruiters search for candidates by name online
  • Personal domains control search results for professional names
  • SEO-optimized portfolios rank higher than resume databases
  • Social profiles alone insufficient for professional presentation

Personal Branding

  • Professionals building brands beyond single employers
  • Portable identity across job changes and career pivots
  • Thought leadership through owned content platforms
  • Network building independent of company affiliations

Gig Economy Growth

  • Freelancers and contractors need professional presence
  • Multiple income streams require unified presentation
  • Client acquisition through professional domains
  • Portfolio-based hiring versus resume-based screening

High-Value Professional Domain Categories

Several categories serve career-focused buyers:

Name-Based Domains

  • Format: FirstNameLastName.com
  • Buyers: Professionals establishing personal brands
  • Value: Permanent career asset, portable across employers
  • Use: Portfolio, contact hub, professional identity

Profession + Name Combinations

  • Format: NameProfession.com (e.g., "SmithDesign.com")
  • Buyers: Professionals emphasizing specialty
  • Value: Clear professional positioning
  • Use: Service marketing, client acquisition

Industry Professional Terms

  • Format: ProfessionalTitle.com (e.g., "Developer.com," "Designer.com")
  • Buyers: Industry associations, job platforms, training companies
  • Value: Category authority and aggregation
  • Use: Professional directories, job boards, resources

Skill and Certification Names

  • Format: SkillName.com (e.g., "PythonDev.com," "UXDesign.com")
  • Buyers: Training platforms, certification bodies, communities
  • Value: Educational and community hub potential
  • Use: Training resources, certification tracking, forums

Valuation Framework

Professional domains value based on career market dynamics:

Professional Demographics

  • High-income professions support higher domain prices
  • Medicine, law, finance, tech have strongest budgets
  • Creative professions value portfolio presentation highly
  • Executive and C-level professionals invest in personal branding

Career Stage Considerations

  • Early career: Lower budgets but long holding potential
  • Mid-career: Strongest acquisition budgets and urgency
  • Senior/Executive: Premium pricing for reputation management
  • Portfolio career: Multiple domain needs for diverse work

Market Comparables

  • Research professional domain sales when available
  • Personal branding service pricing provides reference
  • Resume and career service costs establish baseline
  • Professional website development costs add context

Case Study: Career Domain Success

An investor acquired 50 common professional name domains in 2022 at $15 each ($750 total). Target: Medical and legal professionals in major metros. Strategy: (1) Monitored licensing board announcements for newly-licensed professionals; (2) Sent congratulatory emails with domain offers; (3) Priced at $1,500 with payment plan option. Results over 3 years: 28 domains sold at average $1,650 = $46,200 revenue. 22 domains remaining valued at $1,400 average = $30,800 portfolio value. Total ROI: 10,000%+ on initial investment. Marketing to Professionals

Effective outreach strategies for career buyers:

Timing-Based Outreach

  • Graduation seasons for new professionals
  • Licensing and certification completions
  • Job transitions and career changes
  • Business launches and independent practice starts

Value Communication

  • Emphasize resume alternative benefits
  • Highlight search result control advantages
  • Demonstrate portfolio presentation capabilities
  • Show professional email credibility benefits

Channel Strategy

  • Professional association partnerships
  • Career services and university relationships
  • LinkedIn and professional network outreach
  • Industry conference and event presence

Building Career Domain Portfolios

Systematic approaches to career-focused investing:

Name Availability Research

  • Identify common names in target professions
  • Check availability across major metro areas
  • Prioritize high-income professional categories
  • Document availability for systematic outreach

Geographic Focus

  • Start with major professional markets (NYC, LA, Chicago)
  • Expand to secondary markets with strong professional bases
  • Consider international markets with western professional systems
  • Balance between concentration and diversification

Professional Category Selection

  • Medical professionals (doctors, specialists, therapists)
  • Legal professionals (attorneys, judges, consultants)
  • Tech professionals (developers, designers, consultants)
  • Creative professionals (designers, writers, artists)

Risks and Considerations

Career domain investing carries specific factors:

  • Privacy Concerns: Some professionals prefer anonymity
  • Budget Constraints: Early career professionals have limited budgets
  • Platform Competition: LinkedIn and resume sites provide alternatives
  • Geographic Limitations: Local professionals may prefer local domains

Mitigate through ethical practices, flexible pricing, and clear value communication.

Future of Career Domains

Trends affecting professional domain values:

  • Remote work increasing importance of digital presence
  • Gig economy growth driving portfolio-based hiring
  • AI resume screening making domains more important for human connection
  • Personal branding becoming essential for career advancement
  • Professional verification and credentialing moving online

These trends support long-term appreciation for career-focused domains.
