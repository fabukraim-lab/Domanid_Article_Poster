Numeric Domains: The Liquid Gold of the Internet

By Timothy S. Krause

3 min read

The Universal Language of Numbers

While keyword domains rely on language, numeric domains speak universally. A sequence like "888.com" or "365.com" transcends borders, cultures, and linguistic barriers. In 2026, numeric .COM domains remain among the most liquid and valuable assets in the domain industry. At DomanID, we observe consistent demand from finance, tech, and telecommunications sectors for premium numeric combinations.

Why Numeric Domains Hold Value

Several factors contribute to the enduring value of numeric domains:

Memorability

Numbers are easier to remember than words for many people. Phone numbers, credit cards, and dates are all numeric. A 3-digit or 4-digit domain sticks in the mind effortlessly.

Global Appeal

Unlike English keywords that limit your buyer pool to English speakers, numbers work in China, Europe, Americas, and beyond. This global liquidity drives higher valuations.

Brand Versatility

Numbers can represent anything: dates (365), luck (888), speed (247), or technology (404). This versatility attracts diverse end-users.

Categories of Numeric Domains

Not all numbers are equal. Understand the hierarchy:

3-Digit .COM (NNN.com)

The pinnacle of numeric domains. All 1,000 combinations (000-999) are registered. Values range from $50,000 to millions depending on pattern. "123.com" sold for millions; random combinations still command $50,000+.

4-Digit .COM (NNNN.com)

Highly liquid mid-tier assets. Values range from $5,000 to $50,000. Patterns like repeating digits (7777) or ascending sequences (1234) command premiums.

5-Digit .COM (NNNNN.com)

Entry-level numeric investing. Values range from $500 to $5,000. Good for portfolio diversification and steady appreciation.

Pattern Numbers

Specific patterns increase value:

  • Repeating: 8888, 9999 (highest value)
  • Ascending/Descending: 1234, 4321
  • Alternating: 1212, 8989
  • Lucky Numbers: 8 (Chinese luck), 7 (Western luck)

Regional Demand Dynamics

Different markets value numbers differently:

Asian Markets

Chinese investors drive significant numeric demand. Number 8 represents wealth; 4 represents death (avoid). Domains with multiple 8s command massive premiums in Asia.

Western Markets

Western buyers prefer meaningful numbers: 247 (24/7 service), 365 (year-round), 411 (information). Tech and service companies favor these combinations.

Global Finance

Financial institutions value short numerics for trading platforms, banking apps, and secure portals. Trust and brevity are paramount.

Investment Strategy for Numerics

Building a numeric portfolio requires discipline:

  • Focus on .COM: Numeric value collapses in alternative extensions
  • Prioritize Length: Shorter is always better (3-digit > 4-digit > 5-digit)
  • Seek Patterns: Repeating or sequential numbers appreciate faster
  • Avoid Leading Zeros: 0123.com is worth less than 1234.com
  • Consider Cultural Context: Understand number meanings in target markets

Liquidity Advantages

Numeric domains offer superior liquidity:

  • Faster sales cycles compared to keyword domains
  • Broader buyer pool (global vs. language-specific)
  • Easier valuation based on length and pattern
  • Strong resale market among investors

Risks in Numeric Investing

  • High Entry Costs: Quality numerics require significant capital
  • Pattern Dependency: Random numbers appreciate slower than patterns
  • Extension Risk: Numeric .NET or .ORG values are fraction of .COM
  • Cultural Shifts: Number meanings can change over time

Valuation Benchmarks

Current market ranges (2026):

  • 3-Digit Random: $50,000 - $150,000
  • 3-Digit Pattern: $200,000 - $2,000,000+
  • 4-Digit Random: $5,000 - $15,000
  • 4-Digit Pattern: $20,000 - $100,000
  • 5-Digit Random: $500 - $3,000
  • 5-Digit Pattern: $5,000 - $20,000

Conclusion: Numbers Never Lie

Numeric domains represent stable, liquid investments in volatile markets. Their universal appeal and memorability ensure enduring demand.
