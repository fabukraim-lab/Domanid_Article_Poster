The Gateway to Web4

By Timothy S. Krause

3 min read

Beyond Web3: The Next Frontier

While most investors focus on Web2 and Web3 opportunities, forward-thinking domain investors are already positioning for Web4—the next internet evolution integrating AI, spatial computing, and decentralized identity. At DomanID, we're tracking Web4 development and helping investors acquire domains that will serve as gateways to this emerging reality. This guide reveals what Web4 means for domain investing and how to position portfolios for the next internet evolution.

Understanding Web4 Evolution

Internet evolution follows generational patterns:

Web1 (1990s-2000s)

  • Static websites, read-only content
  • Domain value: Basic online presence
  • Key players: Yahoo, AOL, early e-commerce

Web2 (2000s-2020s)

  • Social media, user-generated content, platforms
  • Domain value: Brand hubs, platform alternatives
  • Key players: Google, Facebook, Amazon

Web3 (2020s-Present)

  • Decentralization, blockchain, token economics
  • Domain value: Bridge between traditional and decentralized
  • Key players: Ethereum, decentralized protocols

Web4 (2026-2030s)

  • AI integration, spatial computing, ambient intelligence
  • Domain value: Intelligent endpoints, identity hubs
  • Key players: Emerging AI platforms, spatial computing companies

Web4 Domain Characteristics

Web4 will value different domain attributes:

AI-Ready Names

  • Domains that AI assistants can easily reference
  • Clear pronunciation for voice-based AI interactions
  • Unambiguous spelling for text-based AI systems
  • Names that work across multiple AI platforms

Spatial Computing Addresses

  • Domains serving as anchors in virtual/augmented reality
  • Names functioning in 3D spatial contexts
  • Addresses for virtual real estate and experiences
  • Cross-platform spatial identity markers

Identity Hubs

  • Domains serving as personal identity endpoints
  • Decentralized identity verification anchors
  • Cross-platform authentication hubs
  • Personal data control and portability centers

Ambient Intelligence

  • Domains for IoT and connected device networks
  • Names functioning in background computing contexts
  • Addresses for autonomous agent interactions
  • Machine-to-machine communication endpoints

Valuation Framework

Web4 domains require forward-looking valuation:

Technology Adoption Curves

  • Estimate Web4 technology adoption timelines
  • Position acquisitions before mainstream recognition
  • Factor in infrastructure development requirements
  • Balance early risk against first-mover advantage

Platform Agnosticism

  • Prioritize names working across multiple platforms
  • Avoid names tied to specific companies or protocols
  • Focus on functional categories versus implementations
  • Ensure cross-compatibility potential

Long-Term Horizon

  • Web4 positioning requires 5-10 year horizons
  • Patience essential for technology maturation
  • Hold through Web3 transition period
  • Position for multiple exit scenarios

Risk Management

Web4 investing carries unique uncertainties:

  • Technology Risk: Web4 may not develop as predicted
  • Timeline Uncertainty: Adoption may take longer than expected
  • Platform Winners: Unclear which platforms will dominate
  • Regulatory Evolution: Laws may affect Web4 development

Mitigate through diversification and long-term capital.
